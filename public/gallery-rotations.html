Raw rotations applies the axial CW transform six times so you see every orientation the math produces — dimmed cards are shape-equivalent to an earlier rotation (translation, not a new shape). Engine cycle is what rotateCW() actually steps through now: shape-equivalent rotations are deduplicated, and within each equivalence class the rotation whose visual centroid (axial r + q/2) is closest to rot 0's is kept, so rotating doesn't push the piece down off-screen.
